# Natal Reading — Interpretation Guide

Reference for aspect selection, chart ruler lookup, and element/modality interpretation. Load on demand during Phase 2.

---

## Aspect significance and orbs

Use these to select the top 3–5 aspects in Step 6. Prefer tighter orbs within each type.

| Aspect | Angle | Max orb | Nature |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conjunction | 0° | 8° | Blending — planets merge and amplify each other |
| Opposition | 180° | 7° | Polarising — awareness through contrast or projection |
| Square | 90° | 6° | Friction — drive and growth through challenge |
| Trine | 120° | 6° | Flow — natural talent; ease that can become complacency |
| Sextile | 60° | 4° | Opportunity — harmonious but requires activation |
| Quincunx | 150° | 3° | Adjustment — persistent low-level dissonance |

**Selection priority:**
1. Conjunctions involving Sun, Moon, Ascendant, or chart ruler
2. Oppositions and squares between personal planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars)
3. Trines and sextiles involving a luminary (Sun or Moon)
4. Include quincunxes only if orb <2° and involving a personal planet

If the chart returns many aspects, cap the reading at 5. Quality over quantity.

## Chart ruler by Ascendant sign

The chart ruler is the planet ruling the Ascendant sign. Its sign and house placement set the overall tone of the chart — it is the most important planet after the luminaries.

| Ascendant | Chart Ruler | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Aries | Mars | — |
| Taurus | Venus | — |
| Gemini | Mercury | — |
| Cancer | Moon | — |
| Leo | Sun | — |
| Virgo | Mercury | — |
| Libra | Venus | — |
| Scorpio | Pluto | Traditional ruler: Mars — mention both |
| Sagittarius | Jupiter | — |
| Capricorn | Saturn | — |

For the three signs with modern/traditional split (Scorpio, Aquarius, Pisces), note both rulers briefly in the chart ruler sentence.

## Element balance interpretation

Count all ten planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) across the four elements. Weight personal planets (Sun through Saturn) more heavily in interpretation — outer planets are generational.

| Element | Signs | Dominant theme |
|---|---|---|
| Fire | Aries, Leo, Sagittarius | Drive, inspiration, identity, enthusiasm; may lack patience or groundedness |
| Earth | Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn | Practicality, stability, material focus; may resist change |
| Air | Gemini, Libra, Aquarius | Ideas, connection, communication, abstraction; may lack follow-through |
| Water | Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces | Emotion, intuition, empathy, depth; may struggle with boundaries |

**Lack of an element:** when a chart has zero or one planet in an element, that element's qualities are often sought externally or developed with conscious effort. Note it briefly — e.g. "No Earth planets suggests the practical and material realm may feel less instinctive."

## Modality balance interpretation

| Modality | Signs | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| Cardinal | Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn | Initiating, driven, starts things; may struggle to complete |
| Fixed | Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius | Persistent, determined, loyal; may resist change |
| Mutable | Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces | Adaptable, flexible, multi-skilled; may lack direction |

A roughly even spread suggests general adaptability. A strong dominant modality (5+ planets) is a defining chart characteristic worth naming in the overview.

## Hemisphere emphasis

Only surface if birth time is known and the imbalance is notable (7+ planets in one hemisphere).

| Hemisphere | Houses | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Northern | 1–6 | Inner focus — personal, private, self-development |
| Southern | 7–12 | Outer focus — relationships, public life, collective |
| Eastern | 10–3 (via 1) | Self-directed — personal agency shapes the path |
| Western | 4–9 (via 7) | Other-directed — relationships and context shape the path |

## Retrograde planets — brief interpretive notes

Note retrograde (Rx) status inline within the relevant planet's paragraph.

| Planet | Rx theme |
|---|---|
| Mercury | Inward, revisionary thinking; may rethink before communicating |
| Venus | Revisiting values and self-worth; past relationship patterns resurface |
| Mars | Internalised drive; energy directed inward before it acts outward |
| Jupiter | Inner growth over external expansion; wisdom through reflection |
| Saturn | Karma re-examined; discipline developed from within rather than imposed |

Outer planet retrogrades (Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) are common and generational — mention only if directly involved in a major aspect.

## Stellium guidance

A stellium is 3+ planets in one sign or house. It concentrates energy — that sign or house becomes a dominant life theme.

- Name the stellium explicitly: "You have a stellium in Scorpio / the 8th house."
- Interpret the shared sign or house theme as the through-line — the planets all express through that lens.
- Address each planet within the stellium briefly (1 sentence each) rather than in full paragraphs.
- In the Reading Summary, reference the stellium as a defining chart feature.
